# Organizations of Purewater

## The Order of the Lake Lady

**Type:** Religious cult dedicated to the lake goddess worshipped in Purewater. They function as both a religious organization and a political entity with considerable influence over water resources and civic matters related to the sacred waters.

**Nature:** The Order maintains the temples and sacred pools throughout Purewater, particularly on Temple Isle. They perform regular rituals to "purify" the increasingly polluted waters and maintain a careful balance between religious devotion and political pragmatism. Members are recognizable by their blue-green robes with silver water-wave patterns.

**Dignitaries by Rank:**
- *Water Initiates* - Novices who learn basic water rituals and serve the temples
- *Reflectionists* - Regular clergy who conduct daily rituals and tend to worshippers
- *Tide Wardens* - Senior clergy responsible for major ceremonies and temple administration
- *Wave Speakers* - Elite clergy who have demonstrated magical affinity with water
- *Deep Visionaries* - The highest ranking priests who communicate directly with the Lake Lady
- *The High Tide* - The ruling council of five Deep Visionaries

**Leader:** High Priestess Lilura Deepcurrent, an elderly woman with piercing blue eyes who has served as head of the Order for three decades. Rumors suggest she may have merfolk ancestry, as she can hold her breath for unnaturally long periods.

**What They Say They Want:** To preserve the sacred waters that give Purewater its name and power, maintain religious traditions, and ensure the Lake Lady's blessings continue to flow through the city.

**What They Really Want:** To maintain their political power and influence as the waters grow more polluted. Many in the higher ranks fear the Lake Lady's power is waning and seek either to reverse this decline or to find alternative sources of power. Some of the more progressive members secretly study the corrupted water magic to understand it rather than simply opposing it.

**Allies and Enemies:** The Order maintains cordial relationships with the Governor and city council, though tensions exist over water management. They ally with the Guild of Watercrafters and have a mutually beneficial arrangement with Ravella. Their chief rivals are the Baron's fire demonologists and others who pollute the waters. They view Crowbill's smuggling operations with suspicion but occasionally make use of his services.

## The Watercrafters Guild

**Type:** Professional guild of artisans and magic practitioners who specialize in water-based crafts and minor enchantments.

**Nature:** The guild encompasses boat-builders, canal engineers, water diviners, weather forecasters, and creators of water-enhanced products. They maintain workshops primarily on Merchant's Isle and Forge Isle, where they create everything from enchanted navigation tools to water purification devices. Guild members wear distinctive indigo sashes with their rank indicated by silver, blue, or mother-of-pearl emblems.

**Dignitaries by Rank:**
- *Apprentices* - Students learning the basics of watercraft and minor enchantment
- *Journeymen* - Qualified crafters who can work independently
- *Master Crafters* - Specialists in particular forms of watercraft
- *Tide Masters* - Elite members who combine multiple disciplines
- *Councilors* - The governing body of seven senior members

**Leader:** Guildmaster Torval Bluehand, a middle-aged human whose hands have taken on a permanent blue tinge from decades of working with watercraft enchantments. He is practical, forward-thinking, and more concerned with innovation than tradition.

**What They Say They Want:** To advance the art and science of watercraft, maintain the quality of all water-related products in Purewater, and ensure fair treatment and payment for guild members.

**What They Really Want:** To gain exclusive control over water-based technologies and magic in Purewater. The guild has been secretly researching methods to manipulate both pure and corrupt water magic for commercial purposes, exploring ways to harness the power from the mixing of lake and sea waters.

**Allies and Enemies:** The guild maintains strong ties with merchants and works closely with the Order of the Lake Lady, providing technical expertise for the maintenance of sacred pools. They have a tense relationship with the city's government over regulation and taxation. They compete with the Dwarven crafters of Forge Isle, though some collaborative projects exist. They view Baron Hanzo's DragonBarge as both a threat and a fascinating technological innovation worth studying.

## The Dragonknights

**Type:** Military order serving Baron Hanzo di Teufel, combining martial prowess with demonological fire magic.

**Nature:** Ostensibly a noble order of knights, the Dragonknights are actually brutal enforcers and raiders who serve Baron Hanzo's ambitions. They wear distinctive armor emblazoned with dragon motifs and practice combat techniques enhanced by fire-based demonology. Their reputation for cruelty has earned them the local nickname "dragonshits" among Purewater residents.

**Dignitaries by Rank:**
- *Ember Recruits* - New members undergoing harsh training
- *Flame Soldiers* - Regular troops forming the bulk of the force
- *Smoke Lieutenants* - Squad leaders commanding small groups
- *Charred Captains* - Veteran officers leading larger detachments
- *Ash Lords* - Elite warriors who have undergone partial demonic binding
- *The Dragon Circle* - The Baron's personal guard and inner council

**Leader:** Baron Hanzo di Teufel officially leads the order, but day-to-day operations are commanded by Commander Vask, an albino human with a raspy voice and burn scars covering much of his body. He is utterly loyal to the Baron and known for his ruthless efficiency.

**What They Say They Want:** To maintain order in the Baron's territories, protect trade routes, and uphold the honor of their order through displays of martial prowess like the tournament.

**What They Really Want:** To serve as the Baron's instrument of conquest, using the tournament as cover to establish a foothold in Purewater. They seek to disrupt the city's connection to water magic and replace it with their fire-based powers, turning Purewater into another territory under the Baron's control.

**Allies and Enemies:** The Dragonknights have few true allies in Purewater but maintain cordial official relations with the Governor as visiting dignitaries. They are actively opposed by the Order of the Lake Lady and viewed with suspicion by most residents. Some corrupt officials and merchants have been bribed to support their interests. Their worst enemies are those who have survived their previous raids and massacres, including Gardwen.

## The Mermaid's Court

**Type:** A secretive society of water magic practitioners with strong connections to merfolk and other aquatic beings.

**Nature:** Operating primarily from Lost Isle and hidden underwater chambers beneath Purewater, the Mermaid's Court preserves ancient water magic traditions predating the city itself. Members include humans, half-merfolk, and other water-touched individuals who practice pure water magic in its oldest forms. They move through the city leaving little trace, recognizable only by small mother-of-pearl tokens they carry and subtle blue-green markings on their skin.

**Dignitaries by Rank:**
- *Surface Swimmers* - Initiates just beginning to learn water magic
- *Tide Dancers* - Regular members who practice traditional water magic
- *Depth Callers* - Adepts who can communicate with aquatic beings
- *Wave Shapers* - Masters who can manipulate water in complex ways
- *Abyss Touched* - Elite members who have undergone transformation rituals
- *The Deep Council* - Seven leaders who guide the society

**Leader:** The mysterious figure known only as "The Pearl" - rumors vary as to whether this is a merfolk princess, an ancient water elemental in human form, or something else entirely. Few have met The Pearl directly, with most orders coming through the Deep Council.

**What They Say They Want:** To protect the purest forms of water magic, maintain connections with merfolk and other aquatic races, and preserve the balance between lake, river, and sea.

**What They Really Want:** To reclaim Purewater from human control and eventually return it to its original state as a sacred meeting place of waters. They believe the pollution and corruption of the waters is not just physical but spiritual, and seek to cleanse the city through incremental changes over generations if necessary.

**Allies and Enemies:** The Court maintains cautious alliance with some members of the Order of the Lake Lady, particularly those who follow older traditions. They have a complex relationship with Ravella, who may have connections to their organization. They are fundamentally opposed to the Baron's fire magic and the pollution of the waters but prefer subtle sabotage to open confrontation. They view most of Purewater's human institutions with patient disdain.

## The Currents

**Type:** A network of spies, informants, and agents operating throughout Purewater's canals and back alleys.

**Nature:** The Currents function as both an intelligence network and a loose association of independent operators who trade in secrets and favors. Unlike Crowbill's criminal organization, they deal primarily in information rather than contraband or protection. They have no fixed headquarters but meet in changing locations identified by subtle chalk marks showing flowing water. Members identify themselves through coded phrases about tides and currents.

**Dignitaries by Rank:**
- *Droplets* - Casual informants who report what they observe
- *Streams* - Regular agents who gather information actively
- *Rivers* - Specialized operatives with particular areas of expertise
- *Confluences* - Handlers who manage multiple agents
- *Deltas* - Senior members who analyze and distribute information
- *The Watershed* - The inner circle who direct major operations

**Leader:** No single leader exists, but the closest equivalent is "The Source," believed to be a consortium of three to five individuals whose identities remain unknown even to most members of the organization.

**What They Say They Want:** To maintain the free flow of information throughout Purewater, ensure no single faction gains too much control, and profit from the secrets they uncover.

**What They Really Want:** To act as the true power behind Purewater's governance, manipulating various factions against each other to maintain a balance that benefits their mysterious leadership. Some suspect they serve an outside power, perhaps from the capital city or even foreign interests.

**Allies and Enemies:** The Currents maintain working relationships with almost every power in Purewater, including Ravella, Crowbill, and elements within the government and religious orders. They sell information to anyone who can pay but are careful never to ally too closely with any single faction. Their primary rivals are other intelligence networks like Ravella's personal agents and the Baron's spies.

## The Dredgers

**Type:** Labor guild and mutual aid society for Purewater's working poor, particularly those who maintain the canals and waterways.

**Nature:** Originally formed as an association of canal dredgers who clear silt and garbage from Purewater's waterways, the organization has evolved into a broader movement representing laborers, fishermen, and the working poor of The Warren and The Shoals. They maintain a guildhall in The Warren and several safe houses throughout the poorer districts. Members wear simple black arm bands with a white wave pattern.

**Dignitaries by Rank:**
- *Hands* - Regular members who pay dues and receive protection
- *Voices* - Representatives who speak for specific neighborhoods or trades
- *Channels* - Organizers who coordinate activities and resources
- *Harbors* - Leaders who manage safe houses and mutual aid
- *Pilots* - The executive committee who guide overall strategy

**Leader:** Marda Blackwater, a broad-shouldered woman in her forties who began as a dredger herself and rose to prominence during a labor dispute five years ago. She is practical, passionate, and utterly committed to improving conditions for Purewater's working class.

**What They Say They Want:** Fair wages, safer working conditions, proper equipment, and respect for the essential work they do keeping Purewater's canals navigable and (relatively) clean.

**What They Really Want:** To transform Purewater's power structure by building worker solidarity across all industries and eventually securing representation in the city's governance. Some of the more radical elements seek to overthrow the current order entirely and establish a new system where workers control the means of production.

**Allies and Enemies:** The Dredgers maintain cautious alliances with progressive elements of the Order of the Lake Lady and some of the more ethical merchants. They have a complicated relationship with Crowbill, who exploits but also sometimes aids the poor. Their primary opponents are the wealthy merchant princes, the Governor, and other elites who benefit from cheap labor. They are particularly hostile to the Baron and his Dragonknights, seeing them as the ultimate expression of aristocratic oppression.

## The Harbor Masters

**Type:** Regulatory guild and administrative body controlling Purewater's docks, harbors, and shipping.

**Nature:** The Harbor Masters control the flow of maritime traffic in and out of Purewater, maintaining docks, assigning berths, collecting tariffs, and enforcing maritime regulations. Their headquarters is a prominent tower overlooking the main harbor, and they maintain offices at all major docking areas. Members wear dark blue uniforms with insignia indicating their specific role and rank.

**Dignitaries by Rank:**
- *Dockhands* - Junior staff who handle basic operations and record-keeping
- *Pilots* - Skilled navigators who guide ships through tricky channels
- *Harbormen* - Mid-level administrators managing specific docking areas
- *Tide Lords* - Senior officials overseeing larger harbor sections
- *Port Captains* - Executive officials responsible for entire harbors
- *The Anchor Council* - Seven masters who govern the entire organization

**Leader:** Harbormaster Velen Deepkeel, a stern older man with a weathered face who rose through the ranks over forty years of service. He is meticulous, traditional, and resistant to change or corruption—at least in his public persona.

**What They Say They Want:** To ensure safe, efficient harbor operations, maintain Purewater's status as a trading hub, and uphold maritime traditions and regulations that have served the city for generations.

**What They Really Want:** To maintain their near-monopoly on maritime commerce and the considerable wealth it generates. The higher ranks of the Harbor Masters have become increasingly corrupt, taking bribes to overlook smuggling operations while maintaining a facade of strict regulation. They participate in complex schemes to manipulate shipping tariffs to their advantage.

**Allies and Enemies:** The Harbor Masters maintain strong ties with the Governor and city council, as well as with wealthy merchants who depend on shipping. They have an uneasy arrangement with Crowbill, whose smuggling operations they officially oppose but secretly facilitate for the right price. They view the Dredgers with contempt and actively work to suppress their organization of dock workers. They are cautiously observing the Baron's arrival, seeing both threat and opportunity in his presence.
