# Purewater: Exhaustive Setting Summary

## Overview

**Purewater** is a Venice-like city built across multiple islands connected by canals and bridges. Originally named for its position where sacred lake waters meet the sea, it has devolved into a hub of corruption and political intrigue. Locals have given it the nickname **"Pisswater"** - a crude reference to the pollution and moral decay that permeates the city.

The city serves as a gateway between inland lake and ocean trade routes, making it strategically valuable and economically significant. This position of power has made it a target for ambitious nobles and a haven for those seeking to escape justice elsewhere.

## Geography and Districts

### High Isle (Main Island)
- Center of official power and authority
- Location of tournament grounds
- Government buildings and administrative centers
- Major temples dedicated to the Lake Lady
- Where the wealthy and nobles reside
- Separated from surrounding islands by Grand Canal

### Surrounding Islands

**Merchant's Isle**
- Guild halls and trade organizations
- Wealthy merchant residences
- Commercial centers

**Temple Isle**
- Ancient religious sites
- Secondary temples and shrines
- Spiritual power center

**Forge Isle**
- Dwarven craftsmen and artisans
- Metalworking and manufacturing
- Quality goods production

**The Warren**
- Poor district
- Working class residences
- Street-level crime and survival economy

**The Shoals**
- Fishermen and dock workers
- Smuggling operations
- Less regulated maritime activity

**Lost Isle**
- Abandoned and partially sunken
- Ancient ruins and unexplored areas
- Mysterious and dangerous

**Pleasure Islands**
- The Sylph's Embrace (Constantine's establishment)
- The Siren's Call (Ravella's operation)
- Pearl Quarter
- Various other entertainment venues

### The Catacombs
- Secret tunnels beneath Purewater
- Used by criminals and for clandestine meetings
- Rumored to contain ancient chambers from before the city was built
- Escape routes and hidden meeting places

## Power Structures

### Official Power
- **Governor/Lord**: Appointed by the capital, maintains nominal authority
- **City Council**: Merchant princes who handle day-to-day governance
- **Temple Authorities**: The Lake Lady's priesthood, spiritual and moral authority
- **City Watch**: Maintains order, often hampered by politics

### Real Power (The Shadow Government)
- **Ravella**: Information broker extraordinaire, controls much of the pleasure trade and information flow
- **Guild Leaders**: Control their respective trades and industries
- **Harbor Masters**: Regulate water traffic and trade
- **Crime Bosses**: Criminal underworld with established codes and territories

## The Lake Lady's Tourney

The annual tournament is Purewater's most prestigious event, celebrating the Lake Lady and hosting competitors from across the realm.

### Tournament Events

**Archery Competition** (Main Event)
- Traditional target archery
- Moving targets on boats between islands
- Cross-channel shots requiring precision and skill
- Prizes: Court access and noble titles

**Single Combat**
- Martial display and dueling tournament
- One-on-one combat matches
- Where the Baron plans to enter his possessed champion
- Prizes: Guild apprenticeships and contracts

**Harbor Run**
- Local obstacle course unique to Purewater
- Swimming, climbing, and boat-hopping through canals
- Tests agility, strength, and local knowledge
- Secretly used for recruitment by criminal and military interests
- Prizes: Cash and local fame

### Tournament Timing
- Preparations and arrivals occur 3 days before the main event
- Registration and competitor sizing-up happens 2 days before
- Main events span the tournament proper

## Magic and Supernatural Forces

### Three Types of Magic

**Pure Water Magic**
- Connected to merfolk and ancient pre-human powers
- Tied to the Lake Lady's blessed waters
- Healing and protective in nature
- Associated with Gardwen and water-based practitioners
- Increasingly rare and suppressed

**Corrupt Water Magic**
- Grey area between pure and fallen
- Used by some city powers who prioritize profit over purity
- Ravella's domain of influence
- Blends natural magic with exploitation

**Fire/Dragon Magic (Demonology)**
- Practiced by Baron Hanzo di Teufel
- Used in possession rituals and binding spells
- Creates steam and heat effects where it meets water
- Corrupting influence on water magic
- Ancient and forbidden in many circles

### The Possession Ritual System
- Uses a demonologist's soul knife (marked with symbols like Asmodeus)
- Requires specific components including bones of the spirit to be bound
- Can bind various types of spirits: ancient warriors, demons, lesser spirits
- Effects range from full possession (complete control) to partial possession (mixed consciousness)
- Key vulnerabilities exist in lockets, remains, and other binding artifacts

## Key Characters and NPCs

### Primary Protagonists

**Gardwen** - The Seeker
- Druid with nature and water magic
- Survivor of Dragon Knight raid (10 years ago)
- Lost her entire family in the attack
- Searching for her brother, kidnapped during the raid
- Companion: Ruhi, a wolf familiar
- Can heal wounds and sense water corruption
- Has never stopped looking for her brother

**Magda** - The Warrior of Hel
- Elderly woman of surprising combat prowess
- Serves the death goddess Hel
- Called to action through prophetic dreams and visions
- Dreamed of the other characters surrounded by fire and dragon figures
- Protective figure and spiritual anchor for the group
- Limited but meaningful prophetic abilities

**Randall** - The Street Thief
- Native of Purewater, raised in brothels on Pearl Quarter
- Orphaned, never knew his parents
- Expert in stealth, theft, and local connections
- Street-savvy and pragmatic survivor
- Secret: Twin brother of Conall, biological son of Baron Hanzo
- His resemblance to Santo creates both opportunities and dangers

**Conall** - The Magical Investigator
- Adopted by a family in Anminster
- Trained by the Order of the Blue Star
- Skilled in arcane magic and investigation
- Fellow thief with Nus Rutas (who will steal the locket)
- Secret: Twin brother of Randall, biological son of Baron Hanzo
- Working with Temerach Nebulo to expose the Baron's crimes

**Temerach Nebulo** - The Lakelady's Javelin
- Elite elven knight of the Swords of the Lake
- Exceptional skill with bow and spear
- Tracking the Baron's crimes and activities
- Member of an order dedicated to protecting water magic
- Has knowledge of the Baron's past misdeeds
- Seeks to expose and stop the "Awake the Dragon" movement

### Primary Antagonists

**Baron Hanzo di Teufel** - The Dragon Master
- Master practitioner of fire-based demonology
- Rising political power in the borderlands
- Seeks political influence through tournament victory
- Ultimate goal: transformation into a Dragon King
- Commands the Dragon Knights (called "dragonshits" locally)
- Arrived in Purewater on the DragonBarge
- Secret father of the twins (Randall/Conall) through rape of an elven mage
- Patient schemer with long-term goals
- Keeps Thuban Eta's bones as primary binding anchor for the possession ritual

**Santo di Teufel** - The Baron's Cruel Son
- Trained in his father's dark arts
- Sadistic and eager to prove himself
- Attempted to bind a pleasure demon into Emmeralda using a ritual knife
- Dropped the knife during escape (evidence of his crime)
- Less skilled than his father but dangerous
- Will pursue recovery of the knife and revenge against those who thwarted him

**Tat Atarer** - Leader of the Dragon Knights
- Former mercenary captain
- Professional soldier and disciplinarian
- Values military efficiency and results
- Loyal to the Baron but not fanatical
- Primarily focused on tournament security and knight discipline
- Skeptical of occult practices but keeps opinions private
- Respected by his men for fairness and competence

**Blau** - Lieutenant of the Dragon Knights
- Albino with distinctive red eyes and pale skin
- Deathly thin appearance but unnaturally strong
- Handles the Baron's "dirty work"
- Cold, calculating, and enjoys intimidation
- Developing fire magic abilities independent of the Baron
- Secret: Studying the Baron's techniques, possibly planning to break away
- Burns scars rumored beneath his gloves
- May have undergone experimental procedures

### Key Supporting Characters

**Thuban Eta** - The Bound Spirit
- Ancient dragon knight spirit from ages past
- Skilled military leader and tactical genius
- Betrayed by treacherous comrades in the Dragon army
- Forcibly bound to Gardwen's brother (Cailan) by the Baron's ritual
- Reluctant participant, held in thrall through possession ritual
- Honors the old codes despite current circumstances
- Still experiences the torment of his violent death and betrayal
- Control maintained through his earthly remains (bones) and the binding locket
- Could become an ally if freed from the Baron's control
- Occasionally fights against the Baron's will, creating moments of clarity in Cailan

**Cailan** - The Possessed Warrior
- Gardwen's brother, kidnapped as a child (10 years ago)
- Now serves as the Baron's tournament champion
- Wears ancient dragon-scale armor that partially fuses with his skin
- Eyes shift between human and reptilian when possessed
- Experiences moments of clarity between periods of full possession
- Developed fragmented dual consciousness from years of sharing his mind
- Combat abilities: Unnatural precision with bow and spear, minor fire abilities
- Knows ancient fighting techniques from Thuban's memories
- Living embodiment of the Baron's possession ritual success

**Emmeralda** - The Partially Possessed Courtesan
- Elven courtesan at The Sylph's Embrace
- Survived Santo's botched binding ritual
- Partially possessed by a lesser pleasure demon
- Healing saved her from complete possession
- Experiences strange behaviors and speaks in bestial voices
- Occasionally asks for "Lord Santo"
- Living evidence of Santo's crime
- Unique case: Interrupted binding creates opportunity for counter-ritual development
- Potential ally if her possession can be addressed and reversed

**Ravella** - The Information Broker
- Proprietor of The Siren's Call (part high-end pleasure house, part information market)
- Half-merfolk heritage kept mostly secret
- Subtle physical hints: faint scales at hairline, slightly webbed fingers, water-reflecting eyes
- Ostracized by pure merfolk society for mixed heritage
- Built information empire to protect waterfolk living secretly among humans
- Maintains veneer of respectability while controlling much pleasure trade and information
- Network includes gondoliers, washerwomen, dockhands - people touching water daily
- Possible access to merfolk paths beneath the city
- Initially cautious with new arrivals
- Can sense threats to water magic and aquatic communities
- Conflicted between profit and protecting water magic from corruption

**Constantine** - Proprietor of The Sylph's Embrace
- Runs the high-end pleasure house on Pearl Quarter
- Protective of her workers and fiercely independent
- Works under Marisette's overall authority
- Her establishment was the site of Santo's brutal ritual attack
- Hiring protection for workers after the attack
- Values loyalty and codes of conduct

**Thorne** - The Storyteller
- Elderly man found most evenings at The Moist Oyster tavern
- Appears frail but maintains extensive network of contacts
- Collects tales and gossip from new arrivals
- Secret identity: Former member of the Order of the Lake
- Survivor of early Dragon Knight raids
- Possessed of penetrating blue eyes that seem too vibrant for his age
- Cane doubles as disguised weapon
- Extensive historical knowledge about Dragon Kings era
- Can recognize the Baron's rituals as attempts to recreate ancient draconic transformations
- Knows vulnerabilities in possession rituals
- Can provide crucial historical context

**Crowbill** - Criminal Underground Leader
- Leader of Purewater's criminal underworld
- Maintains established codes and rules
- Against: Murder of locals, harm to children, disruption of Lake Lady's sacred sites
- Wiry, average appearance, blends into crowds
- Only distinctive feature: small crow tattoo on back of right hand
- Operations: Canal smuggling, gambling dens, protection rackets
- Network of lookouts using crow symbols as signals
- Access to hidden boat paths through canals
- Several safe houses throughout the city
- Initially opportunistic about Baron's arrival
- Grows concerned as Dragon Knights disrupt established equilibrium
- May become reluctant ally

**High Priestess Nerissa** - Temple Leadership
- Leader at the Temple of the Lake Lady
- Middle-aged woman with silver-streaked dark hair
- Wears blue-green robes that seem to ripple like water when she moves
- Can sense water corruption within sacred waters
- Can perform water-based blessing rituals
- Can communicate with water spirits
- Bound by temple law: Cannot directly intervene unless sacred sites threatened
- Needs the PCs as agents to act
- Provides spiritual support, ritual knowledge, and water magic countermeasures
- Tracking increasing corruption since Baron's arrival

## Important Items and Artifacts

### The Ritual Knife
- Demonologist's soul knife
- Emblazoned with the symbol of Asmodeus
- Used to carve ritual marks on victims
- Used to bind spirits to flesh
- Found at the scene of Santo's attack on Emmeralda
- Critical evidence connecting Santo to the crime
- Can potentially be used to understand the binding ritual
- Valuable as both evidence and tool

### The Binding Locket
- Contains part of the magical binding connection to Thuban Eta
- Critical component of the possession ritual
- Will be stolen days before the tournament (by Nus Rutas on Conall's behalf)
- Baron views this as absolutely irreplaceable
- Would "turn the entire city inside out to retrieve it"
- Creates leverage against the Baron if hidden properly
- Damage or destruction might weaken the possession

### Thuban's Earthly Remains
- Bones of the ancient dragon knight
- Stored in Baron Hanzo's tower in the borderlands
- Primary anchor and binding point for the entire possession ritual
- Most powerful component of the binding magic
- Destruction would likely free both Thuban and Cailan
- Inaccessible during the adventure but essential to any permanent solution
- Location known only to Baron's inner circle

### Ancient Dragon-Scale Armor
- Worn by Cailan while possessed
- Partially fuses with his flesh when possessed
- Ancient design from dragon knight era
- Provides enhanced protection and damage resistance
- Partially sentient or magically reactive
- Shifts between solid and partially ethereal states

### The Pearl Quarter

### The Catacombs
- Secret tunnels beneath Purewater

## The Central Plot

### The Possession Scheme
Baron Hanzo di Teufel has orchestrated an elaborate plan to use possession rituals to gain political power:

1. **The Long Game**: Years ago, the Baron raided settlements seeking suitable vessels
2. **Cailan's Abduction**: Gardwen's brother was taken as a child during Dragon Knight raids
3. **The Binding**: Using fire-based demonology, the Baron bound Thuban Eta's spirit to Cailan
4. **Maintaining Control**: The binding is maintained through:
   - Thuban's earthly remains (bones) kept in the Baron's tower
   - A binding locket that will be stolen before the tournament
   - Daily ritual reinforcement
5. **The Tournament Plan**: Use Cailan/Thuban as an unbeatable champion in tournament
6. **Political Gain**: Tournament victory will provide the Baron with influence and power
7. **Escalation**: Similar ritual attempted on Emmeralda (pleasure demon binding)
8. **The Bigger Picture**: The Baron seeks dragon transformation and joining the "Awake the Dragon" movement

### Key Plot Moments

**Three Days Before Tournament**
- Santo's insane attempt to bind a pleasure demon into a prostitute
- Attack on Emmeralda at The Sylph's Embrace
- Ritual knife left at scene
- Healing saves Emmeralda from complete binding

**Two Days Before Tournament**
- Santo seeks recovery of the ritual knife
- Attempts at theft, then confrontation, then potential arson
- Brings him into direct conflict with potential allies

**Day Before Tournament**
- Conall's associate Nus Rutas pickpockets the Baron's binding locket
- Successful theft despite security
- Locket hidden away

**Post-Theft**
- Hanzo discovers the theft
- Launches investigation and purge
- Displays Nus's body as warning
- Becomes desperate to recover locket

**Tournament Opening**
- First public viewing of Cailan/Thuban as champion
- Gardwen recognizes her brother
- Plot comes to climax

## Themes and Conflicts

### Corruption vs. Purity
- Pure water magic threatened by fire magic corruption
- City's gradual moral decay mirrored in water's corruption
- Contrast between sacred tournament and dark rituals being performed
- Water magic as connection to natural, ancient power vs. demonology as artificial control

### Identity and Kinship
- Twins separated by fate, united by blood and circumstance
- Gardwen's search for her brother across a decade of loss
- Cailan's fractured identity as host to ancient warrior spirit
- Characters discovering unexpected connections and relationships

### Justice in a Corrupt System
- Official channels hampered by political considerations
- Criminal underworld has more honorable codes than some officials
- Multiple paths to justice create moral complexity
- Question of what justice means for the possessed

### Fire vs. Water
- Elemental opposition between Baron's fire magic and city's water magic
- Spiritual conflict between demonology and natural magic
- Environmental manifestation in steam where fire meets water
- Geographic advantage to water practitioners in Venice-like city

### Power and Transformation
- Baron's desire for dragon transformation
- Cost of pursuing ultimate power
- Questions about what remains of someone after possession
- Ancient vs. modern power structures

### Duty vs. Desire
- Gardwen's duty to save her brother vs. desire to free the innocent
- Magda's duty to Hel vs. personal compassion
- Thuban's duty to honor vs. use as a weapon
- Characters balancing personal goals with larger responsibilities

- Marisette or Constantine hire PCs to provide extra security
- Follows the attack on Emmeralda
- Direct path to investigating Santo's ritual

### Mistaken Identity
- One PC mistaken for Randall, Conall, or Santo
- Creates immediate conflict or secret information
- Draws PCs into the di Teufel family drama

- City authorities hire PCs to quietly investigate strange happenings

- Any PC with water-based magic senses corruption

- Calls to action from temple or water magic practitioners

### Information Gathering
- Encountering Thorne at The Moist Oyster
- Learning history and context from established residents

### Criminal Underground
- Meeting Crowbill through Randall
- Opportunity for non-official investigation

## Environmental and Atmospheric Details

### Water and Steam
- Sacred lake waters increasingly polluted by fire magic
- Steam rises where the DragonBarge's heat meets water
- Pure waters resist corruption, creating strange patterns
- Merfolk paths becoming less accessible due to corruption
- Gondolas and boats as primary transportation

### The City's Odor
- Mixture of exotic spices from trade
- Less pleasant scents from overpopulation and pollution
- Salt air from the sea mixing with canal water
- Smoke and heat from the DragonBarge and fire magic

### Time of Year
- Tournament season brings increased visitors
- Weather appropriate to location (Venice-inspired, so likely Mediterranean)
- Seasonal water levels affect canal navigation
- Festival atmosphere mixed with underlying tension

### Social Atmosphere
- Excitement about tournament mixed with unease about Dragon Knights
- Rumors and whispers spreading through entertainment districts
- Tension between old families and new power players
- Undercurrent of fear about the Baron's presence

### Supernatural Manifestations
- Water spirits withdrawing from corrupted areas
- Merfolk avoiding the dragonBarge and nearby channels
- Unexplained cold spots where fire magic has been used intensely
- Occasional visions or dreams affecting water magic practitioners
- Animals behaving strangely near sites of fire magic

## Magic System and Rules

### Water Magic
- Connected to nature, healing, and ancient powers
- Practitioners include druids, priestesses, merfolk
- Stronger near pure water sources
- Weakened by fire magic corruption
- Can sense corruption and disturbance
- Protective and restorative in nature

### Fire Demonology
- Unnatural and requiring ritual precision
- Binding rituals require specific components (remains, artifacts)
- Creates steam and heat where it interacts with water
- Corrupting influence spreads gradually
- Requires constant maintenance through ritual reinforcement
- Binding can be disrupted through component destruction

### Spirit Possession
- Range from complete possession to partial consciousness coexistence
- Effectiveness depends on compatibility between spirit and vessel
- Multiple types of spirits can be bound (warriors, demons, lesser spirits)
- Possession creates unnatural abilities in the vessel
- Control maintained through magical anchors and artifacts
