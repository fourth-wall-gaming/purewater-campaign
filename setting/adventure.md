# And Then The Dragons Came
## A Purewater Adventure for Mythras Classic Fantasy

*A knife with demonic markings... A possessed young woman... Twin brothers separated by fate... A warrior blessed by death... And a wild druid seeking her long-lost brother. All converge in the corrupt canals of Purewater during the Lake Lady's Tourney.*

## Prologue

*The events of that day shattered her utterly, fragmented her so that it took her a year to emerge from a feral struggle of daily survival to finally bury the dead, to speak the rites, and to lift her eyes from the bloody ground and see the forest beyond. The dragons had come upon her life with fire and steel in the dead of night, laid waste with the purposeful savagery one only sees from the world of men and left.*

*They were just men, dressed in heraldic armor emblazoned with dragons. They stank of evil and death.*

Ten years later, as the Lake Lady's Tourney approaches in Purewater, those same dragons are coming again. But this time, they'll find resistance waiting.

## Adventure Overview

This adventure takes place in Purewater (locally nicknamed "Pisswater") during the annual Lake Lady's Tourney. The players become entangled in a dark plot involving spirit possession, ancient rivalries, and corrupted magic that threatens to upset the delicate balance of power in the city.

Baron Hanzo di Teufel, a practitioner of fire-based demonology, has arrived on his DragonBarge for the tournament with his knights (mockingly called "dragonshits" by locals) and his son Santo. Their arrival coincides with strange events: a young elven courtesan brutally carved with ritual marks, twin brothers discovering their connection to the Baron, and a druid searching for her brother taken by "dragons" years ago.

## Adventure Hooks

Players can be drawn into the adventure through several hooks:

1. **The Tournament Entry**: The PCs arrive to participate in the Lake Lady's Tourney (archery, single combat, or the Harbor Run).

2. **Hired Protection**: Marisette or Constantine, proprietors of pleasure houses, hire the PCs to provide extra security after the attack on Emmeralda.

3. **Mistaken Identity**: One PC is mistaken for Randall/Conall/Santo, leading to either being attacked or approached with secret information.

4. **The Investigation**: The PCs are hired by city authorities to quietly investigate strange happenings before they disrupt the tournament.

5. **Water Magic Disturbance**: Any PC with water-based magic senses the corruption spreading through Purewater's channels from the DragonBarge.

## Key NPCs

### Protagonists

- **Gardwen**: A druid searching for her brother who was taken during a raid by men wearing dragon heraldry. She can heal with nature magic and has a wolf companion named Ruhi.

- **Magda**: An elderly warrior woman who serves the death goddess Hel. Despite her age, she's remarkably capable in combat and has prophetic dreams.

- **The Twins**: Randall (a street thief) and Conall (a magical investigator) who don't know they're brothers, both resembling Santo.

- **Temerach Nebulo**: "The Lakelady's Javelin," an elven knight of exceptional skill with bow and spear. She has connections to the Order of the Lake and knows more about the Baron than she initially reveals.

### Antagonists

- **Baron Hanzo di Teufel**: A noble practicing fire-based demonology, seeking to gain political influence through the tournament.

- **Santo di Teufel**: The Baron's cruel son who uses a ritual knife in an attempt to bind spirits to his victims.

- **The Dragonknights**: The Baron's personal guard, hardened killers who wear dragon-emblazoned armor.

### Other Important Characters

- **Ravella**: The powerful information broker who runs The Siren's Call. She maintains a veneer of respectability while controlling much of the pleasure trade and information flow in Purewater. Possible merfolk connection.

- **Constantine**: Runs The Sylph's Embrace, one of the city's high-end pleasure houses.

- **Emmeralda**: An elven courtesan who survives Santo's ritual but becomes possessed by a lustful spirit.

- **Ravella**: Information broker with possible merfolk connections who runs The Siren's Call.

- **Thorne**: A veteran storyteller and information gatherer found at The Moist Oyster tavern. Despite his apparently frail appearance, he maintains an extensive network of contacts and knows virtually everything happening in Purewater.

## Part 1: Arrival in Purewater

### Setting the Scene

The adventure begins as the PCs arrive in Purewater during the preparations for the Lake Lady's Tourney. The city is bustling with visitors, merchants hawking their wares, and nobles arriving with their entourages. The air is thick with a mixture of excitement and the characteristic stench that gives Purewater its local nickname.

Describe the Venice-like city with its canals, bridges, and the strange contrast between the opulent High Isle and the seedier surrounding islands. The DragonBarge of Baron Hanzo is docked prominently, steam rising where its hull meets the water, creating an unsettling hiss.

### Initial Encounters

- A commotion as someone is accused of attacking a courtesan at The Sylph's Embrace.
- Dragonknights pushing through crowds, making derogatory comments about "kritch" (elves).
- A half-wild woman with a wolf companion asking questions about men in dragon heraldry.
- Two identical men having a heated conversation in a tavern corner.
- Thorne, the elderly storyteller, collecting tales and gossip from new arrivals.

### The Attack at the Sylph's Embrace

The PCs learn about an attack on an elven courtesan named Emmeralda at The Sylph's Embrace. Someone has carved ritual markings into her flesh, though she survived thanks to a druid's healing. This event serves as the entry point to the larger plot.

## Part 2: Investigating the Currents

### Clues and Investigations

- **The Ritual Knife**: A demonologist's soul knife used in the attack, emblazoned with the symbol of Asmodeus, may be found hidden at the scene.
- **Witness Statements**: Descriptions of the attacker match a nobleman's son seen on the DragonBarge.
- **Possession Symptoms**: Emmeralda exhibits strange behavior, speaking in a lustful, bestial voice and asking for "Lord Santo."
- **Tournament Registration**: Records show the Baron has entered someone in the combat tournament despite none of his knights being skilled enough to compete.

### Factions and Alliances

As the PCs investigate, they encounter various factions:

- **The Pleasure Houses**: Influenced by Ravella, they seek retribution for the attack on one of their own.
- **The City Watch**: Officially investigating but hampered by politics as the Baron is an honored guest.
- **The Temple of the Lake Lady**: Concerned about the corruption of the sacred tournament.
- **The Criminal Underground**: Led by Crowbill, they maintain their own form of justice and have rules about which lines cannot be crossed, even in Purewater.

## Part 3: The Possession Plot

### Spirit Binding

The PCs discover that Santo wasn't trying to kill Emmeralda but to bind a spirit to her flesh as part of a larger plot. The ritual connects to the Baron's plan to use a possessed warrior in the tournament.

Investigation reveals that Gardwen's brother may have been taken years ago for this very purpose - to be a vessel for a powerful spirit that could be controlled through the Baron's fire magic.

### The DragonBarge

The PCs may infiltrate the Baron's DragonBarge to gather evidence. The vessel is guarded by Dragonknights and contains:

- The Baron's quarters with magical apparatus for fire-based demonology
- A training area where a possessed warrior practices combat moves with inhuman precision
- Documents revealing the Baron's political aspirations in Purewater
- Signs of water magic corruption where the Baron's fire magic interacts with Purewater's natural energies

## Part 4: Convergence of Plots

### Tournament Preparations

As the tournament approaches, tensions rise. The PCs learn that the Baron plans to use his possessed champion (potentially Gardwen's brother) to win the tournament and gain political influence.

### Setting the Trap

Taking inspiration from the original story, the PCs may help set a trap for Santo by spreading rumors about a ritual to exorcise Emmeralda, hoping to draw him out. This can lead to:

- An ambush in the misty streets of Purewater
- A confrontation in the catacombs beneath the city
- A public exposé during the tournament opening ceremonies

## Part 5: The Lake Lady's Tourney

### Tournament Events

The tournament provides a dynamic backdrop for the adventure's climax:

- **Archery Competition**: Cross-channel shots with boats moving between islands
- **Single Combat**: Where the possessed warrior is scheduled to compete
- **Harbor Run**: A local obstacle course involving swimming, climbing, and boat-hopping

### Confrontations

Multiple confrontations may occur during the tournament:

- **Santo's Revenge**: The Baron's son seeks to recover his ritual knife and silence witnesses
- **The Possessed Champion**: Gardwen confronts what may be her long-lost brother
- **The Baron's Plot**: The PCs discover how the Baron plans to use tournament victory to gain influence
- **Water vs. Fire**: The corruption of Purewater's natural magic reaches a crisis point

## Part 6: Resolution

### Potential Outcomes

- **Freeing the Possessed**: Both Emmeralda and Gardwen's brother might be freed from their possession
- **Exposing the Baron**: The PCs could publicly reveal the Baron's plots and crimes
- **Tournament Victory or Sabotage**: The PCs might win events or prevent the Baron's champion from competing
- **Water's Purification**: Restoring the balance between the pure water magic and the corrupted elements

### Rewards

- Recognition from city authorities or underground elements
- Prizes from the tournament itself
- Magical items recovered from the Baron's collection
- New allies among the powerful factions of Purewater

## Location Gazetteer

### The Sylph's Embrace
A high-end pleasure house on the Pearl Quarter, run by Constantine under Marisette's overall authority. Elegant with thick walls and private rooms, now the site of a traumatic ritual attack.

### The Moist Oyster
A rough tavern in the Maze district with a suggestive sign depicting a half-open oyster shell. Known for cheap drinks, loose tongues, and the occasional knife fight. A gathering place for information, where Thorne the storyteller can be found holding court most evenings.

### The DragonBarge
The Baron's massive personal vessel that steams where it touches water. Adorned with dragon motifs and guarded by his knights. Docked at a private pier on the eastern side of High Isle.

### The Tournament Grounds
Located on High Isle, featuring:
- Archery ranges facing across canals
- Combat rings for the duels
- The Harbor Run course winding through channels and over rooftops

### The Catacombs
Secret tunnels beneath Purewater, used by criminals and for clandestine meetings. Rumored to contain ancient chambers from before the city was built.

### The Siren's Call
Ravella's establishment, part high-end pleasure house, part information market. Located where High Isle's canals meet the poorer districts. A nexus of secrets and whispers where Ravella holds court and maintains her network of informants.

## Running the Adventure

### Themes to Emphasize

- **Corruption vs. Purity**: The contrast between pure water magic and the corrupting influence of the Baron's fire magic
- **Identity and Kinship**: The twins' discovery of their connection and Gardwen's search for her brother
- **Justice in a Corrupt System**: Navigating between official channels and street justice
- **Water as Life and Power**: The spiritual and magical importance of water in Purewater

### Mythras Mechanics Highlights

- **Water Magic vs. Fire Magic**: Create opposed spell effects that interact in dynamic ways
- **Combat Styles**: Distinguish between the formal training of knights, the street fighting of thieves, and the wild combat of druids
- **Social Mechanics**: Use Influence and Passion mechanics to navigate the complex relationships
- **Possession Rules**: Develop mechanics for possessed characters and exorcism attempts

### Adventure Variations

- **Criminal Approach**: Focus on Randall's underworld connections and a heist-like resolution
- **Magical Investigation**: Emphasize Conall's magical abilities and the metaphysical aspects
- **Tournament Focus**: Structure the adventure around the competition events themselves
- **Political Intrigue**: Highlight the power dynamics between factions in Purewater
