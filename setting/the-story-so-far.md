# The Story So Far

*A prose synthesis of the canonical opening arc — the sixteen chapters of the
novelization* And then the Dragons Came *— rebased to Purewater. This is the
established narrative the campaign begins from; the same beats are logged as
entries in `journal/events.json`.*

---

## The wounds that came first

A decade ago, in a quiet woodland grove, **Gardwen** woke in the dark with the
certainty of evil approaching. She screamed for her people to run, but the
dragons — men in heraldic armor emblazoned with dragons, stinking of evil and
death — were already among them. They took heads, they raped, and in the end
they killed almost everyone. Gardwen survived, fourteen years old and alone, and
her younger brother survived too, in his way: his body was never found among the
dead. The wild did not feast on her family; the ground itself gave her food. The
grove, witness to that barbarity, remade itself into a sacred space around her
grief, and years later the druid **Gleinanion** found her there and named her the
cause of its healing. She learned the old ways — and carried her loss like a
second spine. So it was a shock, a decade on, when a retinue of soldiers dressed
as dragons walked past her on the road.

Far away, an old woman named **Magda** — a warrior who had earned her quiet
retirement tending pigs and crops — began to dream. One does not serve **Hel**,
the goddess of death, without paying in nightmares: a brother and sister torn
apart by reptilian talons; two identical men, back to back, blades drawn, ringed
by fire as dragons wheeled overhead; and always, every time, her own death and
the same backdrop — Vacarus' Square. *"Save these children,"* the goddess rasped.
*"Lay the dragon warrior to rest. Unleash your fury."* Magda woke screaming, her
home in ruins around her, and shouted at the dark: *"Who are you even talking
about?"*

## The road to Purewater

Gardwen tracked the dragon-marked column. Hidden in the heart of their camp, she
overheard two officers — one with a broken, raspy voice — laughing about their
errand: their lord, **Baron Hanzo di Teufel**, meant to walk the length of the
march to win an archery tournament, using *"the boy,"* his secret weapon.
*"Politics, Tat,"* the rasp concluded. *"It's all politics nowadays."* When
Gardwen reached out with her magic, she found only blankness in the two speakers
and an ocean of evil in the men around them — the same men who had murdered her
family. She set out for Purewater, the city of canals, with her wolf **Ruhi** at
her side.

At the city's ford she hesitated, suddenly aware of how otherworldly she looked
to the travelers on the road — until a tiny, ancient woman with piercing blue
eyes straightened from a peasant's crouch, walked over leaning on a war-burnished
staff, and asked, as if solving a years-old mystery: *"Who are you?"* This was
**Magda**, sent by Hel, who had been waiting. She bought Gardwen a cloak to hide
in, petted the wolf without fear, and earned a wary trust.

Meanwhile, two men were converging on the same city. **Randall**, a street-thief
raised in the brothels of the Pearl Quarter, was working the crowds at the city
gate when a guard flanking a disguised nobleman went white with terror and
whispered, *"My lord Santo!"* Randall bluffed past it — but filed the fact away:
his face was worth something. And **Conall**, a militiaman's son turned sell-sword,
turned arcane investigator, rode in beside the elven knight **Temerach Nebulo**,
"the Lakelady's Javelin," who had entered the tournament's archery for reasons she
would not yet name. On the road they passed a sneering, bone-white albino among
the dragon-bannered soldiers.

## The crime at the Sylph's Embrace

Magda took work protecting the courtesans at **The Sylph's Embrace**, the house
of the madame **Constantine**, and brought Gardwen in to stay. Two nights later
the house woke to screams. A man who looked like Randall had carved a long,
curling ritual wound the length of the elven courtesan **Emmeralda**, then fled,
leaving his bloodied, barbed knife behind an armoire. Gardwen's druid magic —
the brooks, the soil, the ancient forests channeled through her hands — closed
the wounds and held the girl to life. *"The woman from the woods,"* the watching
courtesans whispered. Magda gave chase and lost him. *"It looked like Randall,"*
someone said.

So Randall and Conall — strangers who happened to share a face — were both seized
by the mage **Hesper**, Marisette's enforcer, bound to chairs in the **catacombs**, and
brought before **Marisette**, the madame who rules the pleasure trade, accused of the
crime. It was Randall's account of "Lord Santo" at the gate, and Magda's
flat-voiced testimony — *"these boys didn't attack Emmeralda; I chased a richer,
younger man out of the Sylph"* — that saved their throats. Marisette named the
nobleman: **Baron Hanzo di Teufel**. Conall, examining the scene, used his magic
to find the abandoned soul-knife, its hilt marked with the sigil of **Asmodeus**.
Magda recoiled — *"Dark magic, Hel's teeth!"* — and over that cursed blade, four
strangers tied by fate forged an alliance. *"We could. We should." — "Justice."*

## What the night revealed

At the **Moist Oyster**, the storyteller **Thorne** fished their whole story out
of the loose-tongued men while Gardwen stonewalled him to perfection. Randall
struck a pact with the pickpocket **Nus** for intelligence on the Baron's
fortified estate. Alone afterward, the twins half-joked their way to the truth
between them: they were brothers, and the Baron might be their father. And in a
stable the next day, **Temerach** pulled the floor from under Conall entirely —
his mother was an elven mage the Baron had seduced and broken; Conall was Hanzo's
son; that was why she had recruited and trained him for years. She named the
weapon a demonologist's **soul-knife**, used to bind spirits into flesh, and
warned them all: they were playing a deadly game now.

The Baron's heir, **Santo**, came raging down the Pearl Quarter for his lost knife
— *"I'll burn your stupid house down!"* — until the albino lieutenant **Blau**
hauled him back to his father with a slap that cracked across the street. And
Emmeralda's wound began to writhe: she spoke in a bestial voice, begging for "Lord
Santo," and Gardwen's evil-sight revealed a demon coiled in her flesh — the
binding Santo had botched, and Gardwen's healing had only half-undone. **Marisette**
answered by closing the entire pleasure trade to the di Teufel retinue, city-wide.

The arc as the novelization leaves it: the companions set a trap, seeding a rumor
through Thorne that Emmeralda will be exorcised in the woods that night, to lure
Santo and recover his knife. Even the Maze thug **Sinnit** agrees to help set it
up — *"no one does that to our girls."* Ahead lie the theft of the Baron's binding
locket, his furious purge to recover it, and the opening of the **Lake Lady's
Tourney**, where his champion will at last be unveiled — and Gardwen will look
across the tournament ground into the face of her stolen brother.
