# Magic in Purewater

Purewater sits at a nexus of magical energies where the sacred lake waters meet the sea, creating a unique arcane environment where various magical traditions have flourished. Rather than the rigid structure of Classic Fantasy magic, Purewater's magical landscape follows the five traditions of standard Mythras: Animism, Folk Magic, Divine Magic, Sorcery, and Mysticism. These traditions interweave with the city's distinct water-focused culture and the corruption that threatens it.

## Animism in Purewater

Animism thrives in Purewater, centered on the spirits of water in its many forms and the elemental powers that reside in the lake, sea, and canals.

### Manifestations:
- **Water Spirits**: Each canal, fountain, and pool may host its own spirit with unique personality and powers
- **Elemental Beings**: Water elementals of varying power levels inhabit the deeper waters
- **Ancestor Spirits**: Particularly in The Shoals and Lost Isle, the spirits of drowned sailors and lost citizens
- **Merfolk Connection**: Some spirits serve as intermediaries between humans and merfolk

### Organizational Approaches:
- **The Mermaid's Court**: Masters of water spirit binding and communication. Their **Depth Callers** can summon water spirits from extraordinary distances, while **Abyss Touched** members forge permanent bonds with powerful aquatic entities.

- **The Order of the Lake Lady**: **Wave Speakers** work with spirits that embody the sacred lake's purity, binding them to cleanse polluted waters. Their approach is more ceremonial and less individualistic than the Mermaid's Court.

- **The Dredgers**: Practice a pragmatic form of animism focused on appeasing the spirits of the canals they work in. **Harbors** maintain small shrines beneath bridges and teach simple fetish-making to protect workers.

- **Baron's Dragonknights**: Their perversion of animism involves binding fire spirits and minor demons to their weapons and armor. **Ash Lords** undergo partial binding themselves, allowing fire spirits limited access to their physical forms.

### Fetishes and Binding Items:
- Water-smoothed stones with natural holes (for binding water spirits)
- Shells inscribed with spiraling patterns (for communication)
- Bottles of mixed lake and sea water (for summoning)
- Carved coral amulets (for protection from hostile water spirits)
- Canal silt formed into figurines (for minor workings)

## Folk Magic in Purewater

Folk magic is widespread in Purewater, with many citizens knowing at least one or two practical cantrips for daily life in a water-bound city.

### Common Spells:
- **Dry** (essential for life in a perpetually damp city)
- **Purify** (used on drinking water and food)
- **Repair** (for water damage to wooden structures)
- **Warm** (to counter the chill of the mists)
- **Bladesharp** (popular among fishermen for their knives)
- **Witchsight** (to see through mist and murky water)
- **Beastcall** (used by fishermen to attract their catch)
- **Shimmer** (creates dancing lights on water surfaces for festivals)

### Water-Specific Folk Magic:
- **Current's Favor** (helps navigation through tricky channels)
- **Water Whisper** (sends a spoken message along a water current)
- **Chill** (preserves fish and other perishables)
- **Mist Form** (creates concealing mist)

### Organizational Approaches:
- **The Watercrafters Guild**: Folk magic forms the foundation of their work. **Apprentices** begin with basic spells like Repair and Purify, while **Master Crafters** combine multiple folk magic effects into complex enchantments for boats and water tools.

- **The Dredgers**: Focus on practical folk magic for labor and mutual aid. **Channels** teach spells like Dry, Repair, and Warm to help workers endure harsh conditions, and have developed specialized variants for canal maintenance.

- **The Currents**: Employ subtle folk magic for clandestine operations. **Streams** use spells like Muffle, Witchsight, and Water Whisper for gathering information, while **Confluences** teach specialized variants for surveillance.

- **Harbor Masters**: **Dockhands** learn navigation and weather-related folk magic, while **Pilots** master specialized spells for guiding ships through difficult passages.

## Divine Magic in Purewater

Divine magic in Purewater primarily flows from the Lake Lady, though other deities (particularly those associated with water, commerce, and death) also have influence.

### The Lake Lady's Domains:
- Purification and cleansing
- Abundance and fertility
- Healing and restoration
- Prophecy and revelation (through reflective surfaces)
- Protection against corruption

### Other Divine Presences:
- Sea deities worshipped by sailors in The Shoals
- Commerce and craft gods honored on Merchant's Isle
- Death and underworld divinities venerated in relation to the Catacombs
- Hel, the Northern death goddess who sent Magda

### Organizational Approaches:
- **The Order of the Lake Lady**: The primary channel for the Lake Lady's divine magic. **Reflectionists** learn basic blessings and minor miracles, **Tide Wardens** receive more potent healing and purification miracles, and **Deep Visionaries** can call upon major manifestations of the goddess's power, including prophecy and transformation.

- **The Mermaid's Court**: Connect to older, more primordial aspects of the Lake Lady and ancient water deities. **Wave Shapers** can call upon divine powers that predate the city itself, tapping into raw elemental divinity.

- **Harbor Masters**: **Port Captains** and above maintain rituals honoring various sea deities to ensure safe passage. These ceremonies blur the line between divine magic and tradition but occasionally produce genuine miraculous effects.

- **The Dredgers**: Some **Pilots** have begun cultivating a relationship with aspects of the Lake Lady associated with the working poor, developing a more liberation-focused divine tradition that emphasizes community protection and justice.

### Divine Magic Manifestations:
- Blessing or cursing water sources
- Weather alteration (particularly rain and mist)
- Healing through sacred waters
- Visions revealed in still pools
- Protection from water-borne threats
- Creation of magical barriers using sanctified water

## Sorcery in Purewater

Sorcery represents the most intellectualized and manipulative approach to magic, with practitioners reshaping magical forces through will and knowledge.

### Dominant Traditions:
- **Water Shaping Sorcery**: Manipulation of water in all its forms (traditional Purewater sorcery)
- **Demonological Fire Sorcery**: The Baron's corrupting influence
- **Alchemical Transmutation**: Practiced by some merchant guilds for profit
- **Weather Working**: Specialized tradition focused on fog, rain, and storms

### Organizational Approaches:
- **The Watercrafters Guild**: Employs a systematic approach to water sorcery. **Journeymen** learn basic manipulation spells, **Master Crafters** specialize in particular effects like ice formation or mist control, and **Tide Masters** develop innovations that push the boundaries of traditional water sorcery.

- **Baron Hanzo's Dragonknights**: Practice fire-based demonological sorcery. **Flame Soldiers** use simple combat enhancements, **Charred Captains** wield more dangerous fire manipulation, and the Baron himself is a master of complex demonological sorceries that corrupt water and bind spirits.

- **The Currents**: **Deltas** and **Watershed** members practice information-gathering sorceries including scrying, mind-reading, and memory manipulation. Their spells often use water as a conduit or medium.

- **The Mermaid's Court**: **Wave Shapers** blend animistic approaches with sorcerous techniques to create a unique hybrid tradition focused on transformation and communication across different states of water.

### Grimoires and Knowledge:
- "The Flowing Scripts" - Traditional water sorcery texts maintained by the Watercrafters
- "Tides of Power" - Advanced theoretical treatises on water manipulation
- "The Burning Deep" - Baron Hanzo's forbidden texts on fire demonology
- "Mists of the Mind" - Clandestine manual used by The Currents
- "Transformations of the Sacred Waters" - Ancient text predating Purewater itself

## Mysticism in Purewater

Mysticism in Purewater focuses on internal transformation and the enhancement of natural abilities, often drawing metaphorical and practical connections to water's adaptive, persistent nature.

### Common Disciplines:
- **Flowing Form**: Enhancement of movement, particularly swimming and balance
- **Still Waters**: Heightened perception and meditation disciplines
- **Crashing Wave**: Combat-focused mysticism emphasizing overwhelming force
- **Deep Current**: Breath control and underwater abilities
- **Mist Walking**: Stealth and evasion techniques

### Organizational Approaches:
- **The Currents**: Emphasize stealth and perception enhancements. **Streams** learn basic Mist Walking techniques, while **Rivers** and above master complex disciplines that allow extraordinary feats of stealth, memory, and sensory perception.

- **Harbor Masters**: **Pilots** develop mystical disciplines for navigation and weather sensing. Senior members can allegedly predict storms days in advance through meditation.

- **The Mermaid's Court**: Practice ancient mystical disciplines that blur the line between human and merfolk abilities. **Abyss Touched** can hold their breath for hours and swim with supernatural speed and grace.

- **The Dragonknights**: Combine physical training with mystical disciplines focused on pain resistance and battle trance states. **Ash Lords** can enter berserker-like states where they feel no pain and exhibit extraordinary strength.

### Training Methods:
- Meditation while submerged in different types of water
- Breath control exercises in the mists at dawn
- Movement practices performed on rocking boats
- Sensory training in the darkness of underwater caves
- Endurance rituals involving exposure to extreme temperatures

## Magical Conflicts and Interactions

The most significant magical conflict in Purewater exists between the traditional water-based magic (in all its forms) and the corrupting influence of Baron Hanzo's fire demonology. This conflict manifests in several ways:

### Physical Manifestations:
- Steam rises where fire magic meets water
- Polluted channels near the DragonBarge display unnatural colors and behaviors
- Pure waters physically recoil from corrupted areas
- Fire magic appears to "burn brighter" near sacred water sites, as if feeding on them

### Magical Effects:
- Water spells require more effort near areas of fire magic concentration
- Fire magic risks explosive reactions when cast over sacred pools
- Spirits become agitated and unpredictable in contested areas
- Diviners receive conflicting or obscured visions when scrying regions where the magics mix

### Power Dynamics:
- The Order of the Lake Lady's magic weakens as pollution increases
- The Baron's fire sorcery grows more potent as he corrupts more water sources
- The Tournament becomes a symbolic battleground for magical dominance
- Certain locations in Purewater act as magical fulcrums where small changes could tip the balance

## Magical Locations in Purewater

### The Sacred Confluence
Located on Temple Isle, this is where the purest lake waters meet the sea in a carefully maintained pool. Divine magic is strongest here, and the Lake Lady is said to manifest in the waters during high ceremonies.

### The Dragonbarge
Baron Hanzo's vessel serves as a mobile focus for fire demonology. The waters around it gradually corrupt as it remains in place, and steam continuously rises from the hull where it touches the water.

### The Catacombs
Ancient tunnels beneath the city contain pools of water untouched for centuries. These waters have developed unique magical properties, and some believe they connect to primordial water sources that predate Purewater itself.

### Lost Isle
This partially sunken district contains buildings where water has flowed undisturbed for decades. Animists believe powerful spirits have taken up residence here, making it both dangerous and valuable for spirit-binders.

### The Maze
This tangled district of narrow canals creates unique patterns that some believe form natural magical sigils. Folk magic cast within certain intersections is enhanced, and local practitioners carefully guard knowledge of these "power corners."
