# Player-Focused Organizations of Purewater

## The Abyssal Archive

**Type:** Academic institution and magical research collective focused on the study and preservation of water-related knowledge.

**Nature:** Housed in a sprawling building on High Isle that extends several levels below the waterline, the Abyssal Archive contains the most comprehensive collection of texts, artifacts, and magical specimens related to water in the known world. The organization attracts scholars, mages, and knowledge-seekers from across the realm. Members wear deep blue robes with silver trim, the number of silver waves indicating rank.

**Dignitaries by Rank:**
- *Novitates* - Beginning researchers granted basic access to common collections
- *Scribes* - Regular members who catalog and maintain the archives
- *Lorewardens* - Specialists in particular branches of water knowledge
- *Depthsages* - Senior scholars permitted to study restricted materials
- *Tidemasters* - Elite researchers who pioneer new magical theories
- *The Deepcouncil* - Seven senior scholars who govern the institution

**Leader:** Archivist Morien Wavereader, an elderly half-elven woman whose encyclopedic knowledge of water lore is unmatched. Despite her frail appearance, her mind remains razor-sharp, and she remembers every text she has ever read.

**What They Say They Want:** To preserve knowledge of all water-related subjects, advance understanding of water magic in all its forms, and educate worthy students in these traditions.

**What They Really Want:** To reclaim lost and forbidden knowledge about the primordial powers of water that predate current civilizations. The Archive's deepest levels contain sealed vaults with texts deemed too dangerous for general access, including information about cataclysmic flooding magic and water-based immortality rituals.

**Allies and Enemies:** The Archive maintains cordial relationships with the Order of the Lake Lady and the Watercrafters Guild while keeping scholarly exchanges with the Mermaid's Court. They view the Baron's fire demonology as a dangerous corruption of proper magical theory and secretly document its effects. They compete with distant magical academies for rare texts and artifacts.

**Appeal to Players:** Perfect for scholarly character types who want access to rare magical knowledge, research opportunities, and the chance to discover forgotten powers. Characters could rise through the ranks by recovering lost texts, solving magical mysteries, or developing new water-based spells.

**Character Development Opportunities:**
- Research missions to recover lost texts from dangerous locations
- Scholarly duels using water magic to establish academic standing
- Access to unique spells and magical techniques through study
- Politics within the scholarly community as factions debate theories
- Opportunities to specialize in obscure magical disciplines

## The Tarnished Scales

**Type:** Mercenary company and adventurers' guild with a reputation for taking on water-related threats.

**Nature:** Founded by retired sailors and marines, the Tarnished Scales operates from a fortified guildhall near the harbor on Merchant's Isle. They specialize in dealing with aquatic monsters, pirates, smugglers, and other threats that emerge from the waters. Members wear a distinctive scale-pattern armband in copper (which naturally tarnishes to green-blue in Purewater's humid environment).

**Dignitaries by Rank:**
- *Barnacles* - Recruits proving their worth through basic missions
- *Scales* - Regular members who have completed their probation
- *Fins* - Specialized operatives with particular skills (combat, scouting, etc.)
- *Jaws* - Veteran team leaders who command small groups
- *Krakens* - Elite members who handle the most dangerous missions
- *The Captain's Table* - Five senior members who manage guild operations

**Leader:** Captain Dorna Blackfin, a scarred veteran with an artificial leg carved from petrified driftwood. She founded the guild after retiring from the naval service and has an uncanny ability to match members with missions that perfectly challenge their abilities.

**What They Say They Want:** To protect Purewater and its trade routes from monsters and bandits, provide skilled services to paying clients, and create a brotherhood/sisterhood of capable adventurers.

**What They Really Want:** To establish themselves as an independent power in Purewater, eventually securing contracts that make them indispensable to the city's security. The leadership also investigates rumors of a massive sea monster that allegedly sleeps beneath the city, which they fear could awaken due to the increasing magical disturbances.

**Allies and Enemies:** The Scales maintain practical relationships with the Harbor Masters and various merchant houses who hire their services. They have a friendly rivalry with the city guard and a more antagonistic one with Crowbill's organization, whose smuggling operations they sometimes disrupt. They view the Baron with suspicion but would work for him for the right price.

**Appeal to Players:** Ideal for combat-oriented characters or those seeking classic adventuring opportunities. The guild provides a structured way to take on diverse missions while advancing through clear ranks, with excellent opportunities for fighters, rogues, and practical magic users.

**Character Development Opportunities:**
- Combat trials to advance through the ranks
- Specialized training in aquatic fighting techniques
- Missions ranging from monster hunting to escort duty
- Competition with fellow members for prestigious assignments
- Opportunities to establish a personal reputation in the city

## The Mistwalkers

**Type:** Spy network and information gathering service with expertise in stealth and subterfuge.

**Nature:** The Mistwalkers specialize in covert operations, disguise, and intelligence gathering in Purewater's misty environment. Unlike The Currents, who trade in information broadly, the Mistwalkers provide specialized services to select clients, including infiltration, recovery of specific items, and targeted surveillance. They have no official headquarters but maintain several safe houses throughout the city, marked with subtle chalk symbols of curling mist.

**Dignitaries by Rank:**
- *Whispers* - Informants and message carriers
- *Veils* - Trained operatives specializing in disguise and stealth
- *Phantoms* - Field agents who handle complex missions
- *Wraiths* - Veteran operatives who train new recruits
- *Apparitions* - Master spies who design and coordinate operations
- *The Fog Council* - The shadowy leadership whose identities remain unknown

**Leader:** Known only as "The Mist," the leader communicates through written messages and intermediaries. Some believe The Mist is not one person but several who rotate the role, or possibly a retired master spy who never appears in public.

**What They Say They Want:** To provide discreet intelligence services to paying clients, maintain a network of reliable operatives, and perfect the arts of stealth and subterfuge.

**What They Really Want:** To collect compromising information on every significant power in Purewater, creating a hidden archive that serves as both insurance and leverage. Some in the organization believe they should use this information to reform Purewater's corrupt systems, while others see it merely as protection against potential enemies.

**Allies and Enemies:** The Mistwalkers maintain a cautious relationship with Ravella, occasionally providing services while avoiding direct competition. They have infiltrated most major organizations in Purewater, including the Order of the Lake Lady and the Harbor Masters. They actively work against The Currents when their operations overlap and are currently gathering intelligence on the Baron's true intentions.

**Appeal to Players:** Perfect for rogues, spies, and characters who enjoy subterfuge and intrigue. Offers opportunities for stealth missions, gathering intelligence, and navigating complex social situations under false identities.

**Character Development Opportunities:**
- Stealth and disguise training specific to Purewater's misty environment
- Missions requiring intelligence gathering rather than combat
- Politics of balancing multiple clients with competing interests
- Advancement through demonstrating creative problem-solving
- Opportunities to develop unique disguise personas

## The Wavecallers Collective

**Type:** Guild of artists, performers, and enchanters who work with water as their medium.

**Nature:** The Wavecallers create spectacular water-based art, music, and performances that incorporate both aesthetic beauty and practical magic. Their creations range from dancing water displays in public squares to musical instruments that use water's natural resonance to enchanted art that changes with the tides. Their guildhall on Merchant's Isle features constantly changing water sculptures and musical fountains.

**Dignitaries by Rank:**
- *Droplets* - Apprentice artists learning basic water manipulation
- *Ripples* - Journeyman artists who have mastered one form of water art
- *Cascades* - Accomplished artists recognized for their original works
- *Torrents* - Master artists who can create complex, lasting enchantments
- *Maelstroms* - Legendary artists whose works define the collective's reputation
- *The Confluence* - Seven masters representing different artistic disciplines

**Leader:** Maestro Sylvan Tidesong, a charismatic half-elf whose water-music performances can allegedly influence the emotions of entire crowds. His revolutionary techniques combine folk magic, mysticism, and artistic expression into a unique magical discipline.

**What They Say They Want:** To elevate water-based art to its highest expression, beautify Purewater with their creations, and preserve traditional artistic techniques while developing new ones.

**What They Really Want:** To develop water art that can influence the emotions and thoughts of viewers on a massive scale. Some members believe their art could heal the spiritual corruption affecting Purewater, while others see potential for subtle mind-affecting enchantments that could shift political opinions throughout the city.

**Allies and Enemies:** The Wavecallers maintain good relationships with most of Purewater's elite, who commission their works for homes and public spaces. They have a natural affinity with the Order of the Lake Lady, whose ceremonies often incorporate their art. They view the Baron's fire magic as aesthetically crude and destructively opposed to their water-based approach.

**Appeal to Players:** Ideal for creative characters interested in the intersection of art and magic. Offers opportunities for those with bardic inclinations, enchanters seeking a unique specialization, or anyone interested in influencing Purewater through cultural means rather than direct power.

**Character Development Opportunities:**
- Creating signature artistic styles incorporating magic
- Performance competitions during festivals and elite gatherings
- Missions to find rare materials or inspirations for major works
- Balancing artistic integrity with patron demands
- Opportunities to influence public opinion through art

## The Salvage Syndicate

**Type:** Business collective specializing in recovering, restoring, and repurposing items lost to the waters.

**Nature:** The Salvage Syndicate operates throughout Purewater, but especially around the Lost Isle and the deeper channels. Using specialized equipment and magic, they recover sunken treasures, lost cargo, and historical artifacts from beneath the waters. Their headquarters is a massive warehouse on the edge of the Shoals, filled with recovered items in various states of restoration.

**Dignitaries by Rank:**
- *Divers* - Field operatives who perform the actual salvage work
- *Restorers* - Specialists who clean and repair recovered items
- *Appraisers* - Experts who determine the value and significance of finds
- *Brokers* - Members who handle the sale and distribution of salvaged goods
- *Partners* - Senior members with ownership stakes in the Syndicate
- *The Recovery Board* - Five partners who manage overall operations

**Leader:** Vorren Deepfinder, a practical, no-nonsense dwarf who revolutionized salvage techniques by combining traditional dwarven crafting with water magic. His specialized diving bell allows salvagers to reach depths previously thought impossible.

**What They Say They Want:** To recover valuable items lost to the waters, preserve Purewater's material history, and provide fair compensation to salvagers for dangerous work.

**What They Really Want:** To discover and control access to the rumored underwater ruins that supposedly exist beneath Purewater—remnants of an ancient civilization that may contain powerful artifacts and magical knowledge. The leadership believes these ruins could be the source of both Purewater's sacred waters and its current corruption.

**Allies and Enemies:** The Syndicate maintains working relationships with the Harbor Masters (who grant salvage permits) and the Watercrafters Guild (who provide specialized equipment). They have frequent territorial disputes with Crowbill's smugglers, who often use the same hidden underwater routes. They are cautiously interested in the Baron's arrival, as rumors suggest he seeks certain underwater artifacts.

**Appeal to Players:** Perfect for adventurous characters interested in exploration, treasure hunting, and underwater challenges. Offers opportunities for warriors who protect diving operations, rogues who can navigate tight underwater spaces, and magic users who can enhance salvage efforts.

**Character Development Opportunities:**
- Underwater exploration and combat training
- Missions to recover specific items from dangerous locations
- Research to identify and authenticate historical artifacts
- Competition with rival salvagers for valuable finds
- Opportunities to develop or acquire specialized diving equipment

## The Boundary Wardens

**Type:** Protective order dedicated to maintaining the barriers between the material world and water-based otherworlds.

**Nature:** The Boundary Wardens operate on the belief that thin spots exist in Purewater where the material world connects to other realms—elemental water planes, abyssal ocean dimensions, and the domains of ancient water beings. They monitor these boundary points, contain breaches, and deal with entities that cross over. They maintain a fortified chapterhouse on the edge of Lost Isle that resembles a lighthouse but shines its light downward into the waters.

**Dignitaries by Rank:**
- *Sentinels* - Recruits trained to recognize signs of boundary weakening
- *Guardians* - Regular members who patrol known boundary points
- *Binders* - Specialists who can strengthen weakened boundaries
- *Banishers* - Experts who can return otherworldly entities to their realms
- *Planeswalkers* - Elite members who can safely traverse the boundaries
- *The Horizon Council* - Seven senior wardens who identify emerging threats

**Leader:** Warden-Commander Irista Voidgaze, a stern human woman who survived being pulled into an abyssal water dimension for three days, returning with stark white hair and the ability to sense boundary weaknesses. Her experience left her with an unshakable dedication to preventing similar incursions.

**What They Say They Want:** To protect Purewater from extra-dimensional threats, maintain the natural barriers between worlds, and contain any entities that cross over.

**What They Really Want:** To understand and potentially control the boundary points as sources of power. Some in the order believe controlled crossings could grant access to beneficial knowledge and resources, while others fear any weakening of the boundaries risks catastrophic flooding from elemental planes or invasion by hostile entities.

**Allies and Enemies:** The Wardens maintain formal ties with the Order of the Lake Lady, though theological differences create tension. They have a research exchange with the Abyssal Archive focused on otherworldly entities. They are deeply concerned about the Baron's fire magic, believing it may be weakening certain boundaries, and actively monitor the DragonBarge for signs of dimensional disturbance.

**Appeal to Players:** Ideal for characters interested in cosmological mysteries and extra-dimensional threats. Offers opportunities for warrior-mystios who battle otherworldly entities, scholars researching the nature of reality, and magic users specializing in planar magic.

**Character Development Opportunities:**
- Training to recognize and respond to boundary breaches
- Missions to contain entities that have crossed over
- Research into the nature of Purewater's connection to other realms
- Advancement through demonstrated ability to sense and manipulate boundaries
- Opportunities to safely explore other dimensions
