# Purewater: A Conversion from Zorastor
## From "And Then the Dragons Came"

## Overview

**Purewater** is a setting conversion and adaptation of the campaign *And then the Dragons came*, originally set in the city of **Zorastor** in the world of Greymoor. This document outlines how the original campaign's core elements were transformed, relocated, and reimagined for a new Venice-like setting while maintaining the fundamental plot, character relationships, and thematic conflicts of the source material.

## Source Material

**Original Campaign**: *And then the Dragons came* — A Mythras Classic Fantasy campaign set in the southern city of Zorastor (nicknamed "Refuge"), governed by the young and untested Prince Kadith.

**Target Setting**: Purewater — A Venice-like city of canals built across multiple islands, positioned where sacred lake waters meet the sea, locally nicknamed "Pisswater" for its pollution and moral corruption.

## Core Elements Preserved

### The Central Plot

Both versions share the same fundamental narrative arc:

1. Baron Hanzo di Teufel arrives with his Dragon Knights for a tournament
2. He has bound the ancient spirit Thuban Eta to a kidnapped young man (Gardwen's brother, Cailan) through fire-based demonology
3. He plans to use his possessed champion to win the tournament and gain political influence
4. Santo's botched ritual attempt on a woman at a pleasure house provides the PCs their entry point
5. A stolen binding locket becomes the key to disrupting the Baron's control over Thuban Eta
6. Gardwen recognises her brother when the possessed champion is publicly displayed at the tournament

### The Five Protagonists

All five player characters have direct equivalents between the two versions:

| Purewater | Zorastor | Notes |
|-----------|----------|-------|
| Gardwen | Teresa | Druid seeking kidnapped brother; nature magic preserved |
| Magda | Magda | Warrior of Hel; transferred directly |
| Randall | Pete's character | Street thief twin; secret son of Hanzo |
| Conall | Jason's character | Magical investigator twin; secret son of Hanzo |
| Temerach Nebulo | Temerach Nebulo | Elven knight; tracking the Awake the Dragon movement |

The twins' backstory is preserved exactly: both are biological sons of Baron Hanzo, born of an elven mage he raped before establishing himself as a Baron. Their mother died when they were four. They were separately taken in — Conall by Lythande and the Order of the Blue Star, Randall by Jubal's criminal underworld — and grew up unaware of each other.

### The Antagonists

Baron Hanzo di Teufel is preserved wholesale — fire demonologist, Dragon Knight commander, political schemer, with his ultimate aspiration of dragon transformation via the Awake the Dragon movement. Santo di Teufel retains his role and his defining act: the botched attempt to bind a pleasure demon into a woman from the Pearl Quarter, leaving the ritual knife as evidence. Tat Atarer (Dragon Knight leader) and Blau (lieutenant) are both carried across unchanged.

### The Possession Mechanics

The ritual system is identical across both versions:

- A demonologist's soul knife (bearing the symbol of Asmodeus) used to carve binding marks
- Thuban Eta's bones as the primary anchor, kept in the Baron's distant tower
- A binding locket as secondary component, stolen by Nus Rutas before the tournament
- Daily ritual reinforcement required to maintain control
- Thuban Eta a reluctant participant — an honourable warrior betrayed by his own comrades, manipulated through the torment of that betrayal

### The Plot Timeline

- **3 days before the tourney**: Santo's ritual attack on the courtesan; knife abandoned at the scene
- **2 days before**: Santo attempts to recover the knife; escalates through theft attempt, confrontation, potential arson
- **1 day before**: Nus Rutas pickpockets the binding locket at tournament registration
- **Post-theft**: Hanzo launches a purge to recover the locket; Nus's body displayed as a warning
- **Tournament opening**: Cailan/Thuban appears as champion; Gardwen recognises her brother

## Significant Changes and Adaptations

### Geographic Setting

**Zorastor** is an inland southern city under the nominal rule of Prince Kadith. Crime-ridden and reform-resistant, with the Grimguard military police driving criminal gangs underground but failing to capture thief-guild leader Jubal and his Talons.

**Purewater** is a Venice-like archipelago city built across multiple islands connected by canals and bridges, positioned at the strategic junction of inland lake trade and ocean routes. Power is diffused across merchant guilds, the Temple of the Lake Lady, criminal networks, and information brokers rather than held by a single governing figure.

The water-based geography creates a natural elemental conflict with the Baron's fire magic (steam where the DragonBarge meets the water, spreading corruption through the channels), enables distinctive action set-pieces (canal chases, the Harbor Run obstacle course), and makes the Baron's presence felt environmentally throughout the city.

The pleasure district itself is renamed: the Zorastor draft's **Street of Red Lanterns** (a Thieves' World / Sanctuary throwback) becomes Purewater's pleasure island, **the Pearl**, its waterfront the **Pearl Quay** — lamplight strung over black canal water rather than red lanterns over a dusty street.

> **Layout reorganization (later revision).** The geography was subsequently reworked from a concentric ring-city into a **strait-city across "the Mouth"** (where the Lake drains to the Sea): a triangular **High Isle** between two land-jaws (the **Caravan Square** promontory west, the **Shoals** dock-peninsula east), with the **Northern Isles** (the Pearl, Lost Isle, court retreats) scattered north and **Temple Isle** unbridged off the north-east. **Merchant's Isle** and **Forge Isle** became *quarters of High Isle*; the **Maze** and the **Warren** were collapsed into one poor-and-criminal quarter renamed **the Lullwater**. The **canonical layout lives in `locations/` and `lore/geography/`** — the other documents in this `setting/` folder predate the reorganization and describe the earlier, more concentric draft.

### Magical Framework

The original uses standard Mythras Classic Fantasy magic — Arcane, Divine, and Animism systems — with fire demonology present but not foregrounded as an antagonist to a specific opposing force.

The conversion introduces a three-tier water magic system: pure water magic connected to merfolk and the Lake Lady; corrupt water magic in the grey zone; and fire/dragon demonology as active corruptor. Gardwen's druidic abilities are explicitly tied to pure water magic. The DragonBarge physically corrupts the channels around it.

### Political Structure

In Zorastor, Prince Kadith holds nominal power, Jubal's Talons are the primary criminal force, and Lythande and the Order of the Blue Star represent institutional magical authority.

In Purewater, power is distributed across several roughly balanced factions — Ravella's information network, Crowbill's criminal underworld, High Priestess Nerissa's temple authority, and guild and harbor masters. More faction entry points gives PCs with different backgrounds multiple paths into the investigation.

### The Tournament

Prince Kadith's personal showcase tournament becomes the Lake Lady's Tourney — an annual sacred event predating the current political order, featuring archery, single combat, and the unique Harbor Run (a canal obstacle course used covertly for criminal and military recruitment). The tournament's sacred character means the Baron's manipulation of it is a desecration, not merely a political manoeuvre.

### Secondary Characters

Several original NPCs are consolidated, renamed, or expanded:

## Thematic Evolution

### Preserved Themes

- **Justice vs. corruption**: Official channels are compromised; the criminal underworld often has more functional ethics than the authorities
- **Identity and kinship**: The twins' gradual discovery of their connection and shared parentage
- **Duty and prophecy**: Magda's visions and her obligation to Hel

### New or Strengthened Themes

- **Corruption vs. purity**: The water/fire conflict gives this a physical and magical dimension — the city's sacred waters are being literally corrupted by the Baron's presence
- **Possession and identity**: What remains of a person after years of shared consciousness? Can Cailan be restored? Is Thuban Eta a victim or a threat?
- **Ancient vs. modern power**: The Awake the Dragon movement attempts to revive a pre-existing draconic order; water magic represents older, pre-human powers resisting this

---

## What the Conversion Preserves Most Importantly

The emotional core of the original campaign remains intact:

1. Gardwen's decade of searching for her brother ends with recognition across a tournament ground
2. The twins must reckon with a parentage that makes the villain their father
3. Thuban Eta is not simply a possessing monster but a trapped, honourable figure who might become an ally
4. The locket and the bones are the key — not a direct confrontation with the Baron, but dismantling the ritual that gives him his power
5. Magda is there because Hel said so, and Hel is usually right about these things
