Purewater is a city of canals and bridges built across a scatter of islands where the sacred waters of the lake pour out to meet the sea. Named for that holy confluence, it has rotted into a hub of corruption and intrigue, and locals now call it **"Pisswater"** for its pollution and moral decay. It is the gateway between inland lake-trade and ocean routes, which makes it rich, strategic, and a magnet for ambitious nobles and fugitives from justice elsewhere.

Power is diffuse -- no single ruler but a Governor appointed from the capital, a council of merchant princes, the [[The Order of the Lake Lady]], the guilds and harbor masters, the broker [[Ravella]], and [[Crowbill]]'s underworld, all in uneasy balance. Gondolas and boats are the arteries; mist, salt air, exotic spice, and canal-stink fill the nose. Beneath it all runs the elemental tension of the campaign: the Lake Lady's pure waters against the fire-corruption seeping from the Baron's [[The DragonBarge]], steam rising wherever the two meet.

**Islands:** [[High Isle]] (power, the tourney), Merchant's Isle, [[Temple Isle]], [[Forge Isle]] (Dwarven crafters), [[The Warren]] (the poor), [[The Shoals]] (docks, smuggling), [[Lost Isle]] (sunken ruins), and the Pleasure Islands of the Pearl Quarter. Below: [[The Catacombs]].
