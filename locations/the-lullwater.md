Across the canal from [[High Isle]]'s residential north-east edge, sloping
south-east toward the docks of [[The Shoals]], lies the **Lullwater** -- a low,
crowded, water-logged quarter of the **narrowest, most crooked canals** in the city
and tenements leaning over them until they almost touch overhead. Its name sounds
gentle -- a lull, a calm, still water -- and that is the joke of it: the Lullwater
is where Purewater's **underworld and its working poor live cheek by jowl**, thieves
and dredgers and smugglers and dockhands all crammed into the same drowned maze.

It is easy to get lost here, and dangerous to *look* lost -- [[Conall]] learns as
much when he is cornered within an hour of wandering in. [[Crowbill]]'s people move
the canals by hidden boat-paths; the honest [[The Dredgers]] keep their **Dredgers'
Hall** and safe houses here; and the storyteller [[Thorne]] holds court most
evenings at the **[[The Moist Oyster]]** tavern. The quarter's nearness to the
smuggling jetties of the Shoals is no accident -- contraband flows from one straight
into the other.

> *(This district collapses the older "Maze" and "Warren" into one: the criminal
> tangle and the labouring slum are the same drowned quarter. The lovely name is a
> deliberate, ironic mask.)*
