> *"Save the world a tragedy at a time -- and take no credit for it."*
> -- the first article of the Empty Hand

**GM note: the Quiet Hand is a secret.** The players will not find its name in
any archive; most of Purewater has never heard of it. It is recorded here as a
hidden patron and a long lever on the campaign.

## What it is

The Quiet Hand is a fellowship of spies, scholars, healers, diplomats, and a few
mages who share one conviction: that the great evils of the world are best stopped
not by armies or open sorcery, but by **knowing of them first and acting before
they ripen** -- a planted word, an intercepted letter, a witness brought to the
right magistrate, a frightened heir reached the night before the coup. They hold
that history turns on small, almost invisible moments -- they call each one a
**hinge** -- and that whoever sees the hinge first and leans on it gently can bend
the future without ever taking a throne.

They are not the [[The Currents]]. The Currents sell secrets to whoever pays and
keep the city's powers in a profitable balance; the Hand takes no fee, serves no
faction, and spends its secrets to *prevent* harm. Where the Currents are a market,
the Hand is a conscience -- and a far older, far quieter thing.

## Origin (GM)

The Hand was founded generations ago by survivors of a catastrophe they could have
turned aside had they only known in time and dared to act unseen -- by their own
telling, the ruin left in the long fall of the **Dragon Kings**. They swore to
become the thing that strangles the next such evil in its cradle. This makes the
**[[The Awake the Dragon Movement]]** their oldest and most personal nightmare: the
one catastrophe they exist, above all, to prevent from happening twice.

## How it works -- the metaphor of the hand

The order is built so that no captured member can betray more than a fingertip:

- A single agent is a **finger**.
- A few fingers who work together, knowing only one another, are a **hand** (a cell).
- A handler who runs a hand is a **wrist**.
- The leadership -- unknown even to most wrists -- is the **Palm**.

Members carry no insignia and keep no hall. They know one another by **counter-sign**
(one speaks half of an old couplet; the other completes it) and a brief gesture --
an open, empty palm shown for a heartbeat, the sign of the Empty Hand. There is no
roster; there is no banner to capture.

## Method and creed

They prize the bloodless solution above all: the forged writ, the bought hesitation,
the timely rescue, the rumor that empties a square before the soldiers arrive, the
healer who reaches the right bedside. They **kill rarely and reluctantly, never for
convenience**, and they will not seize power for themselves -- power is precisely
the corruption they guard against, so they always act *through* others and then
vanish. Their successes are invisible by design: the war that never started, the
plague quietly contained, the tyrant who inexplicably lost his nerve. The measure of
their work is **absence**.

Mages among them are a prized minority -- valued less for force than for what they
can *learn* and *carry*: farsight and scrying, sendings, disguise, wards, the
occasional decisive word. Every member swears the **Oath of the Empty Hand**: take
no throne, title, land, credit, or monument; act never for yourself; spare whom you
can; carry the light, and pass it on.

## In Purewater

The Hand has one known finger in the city: **[[Hesper]]**, who for years has worn
the mask of [[Marisette]]'s cold, dangerous "enforcer." It is perfect cover -- a
listening post at the very heart of the Pearl's secrets, beside a woman who
hears everything. Hesper has watched [[Baron Hanzo di Teufel]] since before he ever
reached Purewater, and the seizing of [[Conall]] and [[Randall]] into the catacombs
was the Hand controlling a volatile night and getting at the truth -- and keeping
the soul-knife out of worse hands -- not the cruelty it appeared to be.

For the party, this means a **secret patron**. If they prove themselves against the
Baron, a door will quietly open, a guard will be looking the wrong way, the thing
they need will already be where they need it. They may, in time, learn whose hand
arranged their luck. Or they may never know -- which is exactly how the Hand prefers
it.
