Born as an association of canal dredgers who clear silt and refuse from the waterways, now a broad movement of laborers, fishers, and the working poor of [[The Warren]] and [[The Shoals]]. They keep a guildhall in the Warren and safe houses across the poor districts, and wear plain black armbands marked with a white wave.

**Ranks:** Hands, Voices, Channels, Harbors, and an executive committee of Pilots.

**Leadership.** [[Marda Blackwater]] -- a broad-shouldered woman in her forties who rose from the dredge-boats during a labor dispute five years ago; practical, fiery, and devoted to the working class.

**Stated aim:** fair wages, safe conditions, respect for essential work. **Real aim:** to remake Purewater's power structure through worker solidarity and a seat in its governance -- the radical wing wants to overthrow the order entirely. Cautiously allied with the progressive wing of the [[The Order of the Lake Lady]] and ethical merchants; complicated toward [[Crowbill]]; and bitterly hostile to the Baron's Dragon Knights as the purest face of aristocratic oppression.
