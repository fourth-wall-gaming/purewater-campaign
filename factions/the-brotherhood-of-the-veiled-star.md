A hidden, far older thing than any city college of magic: the Brotherhood of the
Veiled Star is a society of wandering sorcerer-adepts who owe no master, keep no
hall, and answer to no crown. They are known -- when they are known at all -- by
the **star that blazes at the brow** when their magic rises ([[Hesper]]'s burns
blue). They drift between cities, trade in oaths and secrets, and are very, very
hard to compel.

**The oath and the secret.** Admission to the Brotherhood is a binding pact that
costs each adept a single guarded **secret**. So long as that secret is never
discovered and spoken aloud by another, the adept's power holds. Should it ever be
uncovered and named, the magic -- and very often the adept's life -- unravels.
This makes the Brethren paranoid, deniable, and dangerous: they guard their own
secrets ferociously and collect everyone else's as currency and armor.

**Not the Order of the Blue Star.** The two are constantly confused, precisely
because both are star-marked -- a confusion the Brotherhood is happy to cultivate.
But the [[The Order of the Blue Star]] is an *institution*: a college of mages with
ranks, a hall, and a doctrine of keeping power out of the wrong hands (it schooled
[[Conall]] through Old Johz). The Brotherhood of the Veiled Star is a *secret
society of oath-bound wanderers* -- no ranks worth the name, no fixed loyalty, and
a vulnerability sewn into every member.

**In Purewater.** The Brotherhood has exactly one known presence in the city:
[[Hesper]], arcane right hand to the madame [[Marisette]] in the Pearl Quarter,
who can drop a strong man with a spoken word. Why a wandering star-adept has tucked
into the politics of a pleasure district is an open question -- a quarter built on
secrets may be the safest place to hide one of your own. Hesper is a wild card:
useful to the party (it was Hesper who recognized the soul-knife's work and may
advise on lifting Emmeralda's demon), but bound to no one's cause but the
Brotherhood's -- and Hesper's own.
