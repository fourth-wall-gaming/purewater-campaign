**GM ONLY.** The hidden architecture of the campaign.

**The scheme.** [[Baron Hanzo di Teufel]] is a fire-demonologist pursuing, ultimately, his own transformation into a Dragon King through the [[The Awake the Dragon Movement]]. His proof-of-concept is a possession ritual: years ago his Dragon Knights kidnapped a child during a border raid -- **[[Gardwen]]'s brother** -- and the Baron bound the spirit of the ancient dragon-knight **[[Thuban Eta]]** into him. That boy is now **[[Cailan]]**, the Baron's unbeaten champion, whom he means to field in the tourney's Single Combat to win political influence in Purewater.

**The binding.** Control is held by three things: **Thuban's bones** (the primary anchor, kept in the Baron's distant border tower, inaccessible during the adventure but essential to any permanent solution); a **binding locket** (a secondary component the Baron carries); and **daily ritual reinforcement**. Thuban is a reluctant, honorable captive -- betrayed and slain by his own comrades in the old Dragon army, now enslaved through the torment of that betrayal. He occasionally fights the Baron's will, surfacing as Cailan's moments of clarity. **Free him, and a betrayed knight may become an ally.**

**The twins.** [[Randall]] and [[Conall]] are identical twins and **both biological sons of Baron Hanzo**, born of an elven mage he seduced and broke long before his open descent into evil; she fled to the city and died when they were small. Separated, they grew up unaware of each other -- Randall in the Pearl' brothels, Conall adopted by a militiaman's family in Anminster and later trained by the [[The Order of the Blue Star]]. The Baron's legitimate, cruel heir **[[Santo di Teufel]]** is their half-brother -- which is why the Dragon Knight at the gate mistook Randall for "Lord Santo." [[Temerach Nebulo]] has known Conall's parentage for years; that is the real reason she brought him.

**The lever.** The campaign's resolution is **not** a frontal fight with the Baron but the dismantling of his ritual. The plot turns on the locket: [[Nus]] pickpockets it before the tourney on Conall's behalf; the Baron, discovering the theft, launches a purge to recover it and displays Nus's body as a warning -- "he would turn the entire city inside out to retrieve it." Hold the locket, and you have leverage; damage it and the bones, and the possession weakens. The emotional climax is Gardwen recognizing her brother across the tournament ground.
