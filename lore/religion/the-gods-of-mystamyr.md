**The Lake Lady (Nimue).** Purewater's patron: goddess of the sacred waters, of purification and abundance, healing and prophecy-in-reflection, and protection against corruption. Her [[The Order of the Lake Lady]] tends the temples and sacred pools; her knights, the [[The Swords of the Lake]], are held to account by the goddess herself. As the city's waters foul, her power is felt to wane.

**Hel.** The death-goddess of the Northern barbarians, grim and terrible. It is Hel who sends [[Magda]] -- through jarring, violent nightmares ending always in Magda's own death -- to protect the four companions and "lay the dragon warrior to rest." One does not serve the Goddess of Death without dread, and Magda curses her patroness even as she obeys.

**Vacarus.** The god of war, invoked by the city's people in oaths and exclamations ("Vacarus knows"); the central square of the old city bore his name in Magda's recurring vision.

**Asmodeus.** The lord devil whose runic sigil is emblazoned on the demonologist's **soul-knife** -- the instrument [[Santo di Teufel]] used to carve binding marks into [[Emmeralda]]. His mark on the blade is the unmistakable signature of the fire-demonology at the campaign's heart, and the sight of it makes even Hel's warrior recoil.

Lesser invocations heard on the street include **Nilo Valis**, patron spirit of thieves and quick improvisation, and the sea-gods of the Shoals and craft-gods of Merchant's Quarter.
