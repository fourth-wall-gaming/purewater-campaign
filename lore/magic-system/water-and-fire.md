Purewater sits at a nexus where sacred lake meets sea, and its magic is organized around a single great conflict: **water against fire**.

**Pure water magic** -- the oldest power, tied to the merfolk, the Lake Lady, and the blessed waters. Healing and protective; stronger near pure water; able to sense corruption and disturbance. [[Gardwen]]'s druidic gift is explicitly of this kind, as is the divine power of [[High Priestess Nerissa]] and the [[The Order of the Lake Lady]]. It is increasingly rare and suppressed as the waters foul.

**Corrupt water magic** -- the grey zone, where practitioners blend natural magic with exploitation and profit over purity. [[Ravella]]'s domain shades into this.

**Fire / dragon demonology** -- the Baron's art: unnatural, ritual-precise, and corrupting. It binds spirits and demons into flesh, creates steam and heat where it meets water, and spreads its corruption gradually through the channels (most visibly from [[The DragonBarge]]). It is ancient and forbidden, the magic of the Dragon Kings reborn.

**In Classic Fantasy (CFI) terms.** This campaign is run under Classic Fantasy Imperative: the protagonists are Cleric, Fighter, Magic-User, and Rogue. The Lake Lady's water-priests are **Clerics** (divine/Theism, Piety and Exhort, water-aspected miracles -- blessing, healing, cleansing, sensing corruption). Arcane practitioners like [[Conall]] and the [[The Order of the Blue Star]] are **Magic-Users** (Arcane Casting -- illusion, invisibility, alteration of appearance, divination/find, counterspell). The Baron and his son work fire **demonology** as forbidden Magic-User/ritual art. Light innate gifts (Ravella's water-sense, a fisherman's cantrips) read as **Folk Magic**. Mages cannot cast effectively in armor.

**Manifestations of the conflict:** steam where fire meets water; channels near the DragonBarge running unnatural colors; pure waters recoiling from corrupted areas; water spirits withdrawing; merfolk paths closing; cold spots where fire magic has been worked; and water-magic practitioners troubled by visions. The Tournament becomes the symbolic battleground for which magic will hold the city.

*(In the original Mythras telling these traditions are Animism, Folk Magic, Divine, Sorcery, and Mysticism, each with its own faction practitioners -- the Mermaid's Court spirit-binders, the Watercrafters' water-sorcery, the Currents' scrying, the Dragonknights' Ash Lords. The CFI port maps these onto the four classes; the flavor of each faction's craft is preserved in their entries.)*
