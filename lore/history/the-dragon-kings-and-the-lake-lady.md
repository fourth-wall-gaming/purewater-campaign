In an age before the present order, the **Dragon Kings** ruled -- a draconic power of fire and dominion, served by an order of dragon-knights. They were thrown down in a great **uprising**, in which the [[The Swords of the Lake]] won their oldest battle honors. The realm of Mystamyr has remembered the Dragon Kings as a destroyed evil ever since; their heraldry is an unwelcome thing to see flying on a border lord's banners.

Older still are the **pre-human water-powers** -- the merfolk, the spirits of lake and sea, and the goddess the city calls the **Lake Lady**. These older powers resisted the draconic order then, and the water-faiths hold that they resist it now. The campaign's deep conflict is precisely this: the Baron's [[The Awake the Dragon Movement]] is an attempt to *revive* the Dragon Kings' transformations -- and his possession of the ancient dragon-knight spirit [[Thuban Eta]] is a first, monstrous proof that the old draconic magic can be made to work again.

This is the history the storyteller [[Thorne]] secretly carries, and why he -- a former Order of the Lake initiate -- can recognize the Baron's rituals for what they truly are.
